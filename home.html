Social Media Is a Trap.

Help spread awareness for the damage Social media can do to your brain.

If you still have active social media accounts, you can help spread awareness to other users TODAY. Make posts linking back to this site, and other websites with similar goals. If you aren’t sure what to post, consider using short segments of the videos below or from This playlist.


Why scrolling on social media is addictive | Washington Post

Summary: Luis Velarde explores what makes using social media addictive and how it may affect a users' wellbeing. Velarde argues that social media use human connections as a drug by honing its addictive nature. They explain how social media influences the release of dopamine, leading to addictive behavior. Further contributors to the addictive nature of social media include its easy accessibility, practically infante content, and the novelty of that content. Velarde refers to social media as a contributing factor to the growing youth mental health crisis, citing the U.S. Surgeon General’s advisory on social media and youth mental health.

Social Media Addiction: The Story and Solutions | Dr Christian Heim

Summary: Psychiatrist Dr. Christian Heim's discusses the negative impacts of social media on the brain. Social media affects empathy, creativity, and reduces the ability to engage with people. Oxytocin, serotonin, and beta-endorphin levels lower as a result of heavy use. Dr. Heim recommends possible changes to combat social media addiction. These changes include more practicing discipline, physical activity, focus on real world interactions, and taking regular breaks from technology.

SOCIAL MEDIA ADDICTION | Leslie Coutterand | TEDxMarin

Summary: Bailey Parnell discusses the 4 main stressors within social media, and how to avoid the negative effects social media can have on mental health. The 4 main stressors Parnell mentions are, the highlight reel, social currency, FOMO (fear of missing out), and online harassment. Parnell recommends monitoring social media use and makes you feel as well as avoiding online toxicity.
